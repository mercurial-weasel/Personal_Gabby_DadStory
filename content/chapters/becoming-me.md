The move to the city was terrifying. I lived in a tiny apartment where the heater sounded like a jet engine, but I felt like a king.

I spent my twenties working three jobs and trying to figure out if I was an artist or an engineer. Eventually, I realized I could be both. I learned that failure isn't the opposite of success; it's just the rough draft.
